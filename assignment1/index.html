What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I noticed in the website is the full sized, animated loading screen with the text "LOADING" displayed in the center of the page. Upon the loading of the page, the page transforms into a colourful splash page with a large, contrasting title and a smaller subtitle to draw the attention of the viewer.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

The main interaction of the site is scrolling. The website prompts the user to do that by making the elements on the site move as the viewer scrolls. Other interactions on the site also includes clicking of certain elements as some elements are deliberately clickable in order to add more value to the site. There is also a fair bit of reading involved as the site has lots of text content to back up the visuals.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

At one point there was this large heart-shaped button that was pulsating, prompting me to take some form of action on it. So i clicked on it. Upon clicking on it, more hearts appeared on the page. I spent a good few minutes clicking in order to see if anything special happens after a certain number of clicks or if there was any restriction on the number of hearts.

What was the most common action in your two-minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action was scrolling. The animations and elements on this webpage are all bounded together by scrolling. So most of this webpage is just scrolling, watching change in content, and reading, before resuming scrolling.

The usage of bright, contrasting colours and large, bold fonts used for the titles assisted greatly in understanding the intentions and goals of this webpage. The description texts in the webpage is also kept deliberately short and digestable for quick reading.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The elements in the page visually changes and moves around as the viewer scrolls down, giving the viewers a visual prompt to how much more they have to scroll to reach the next segment of the webpage. Once the viewer reaches the intended segment, the moving elements stop moving and textual content starts to appear, signalling to the viewer to stop scrolling and read the content.
